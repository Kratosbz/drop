General
7 questions
What is Grass?
Grass is a network of over 2 million people who are rewarded for helping AI developers access the internet. Its goal is to prevent the concentration of AI by allowing ordinary people to play a part and share in the benefits of its growth.

Since everyone already pays for internet, why not put your idle connection to work? Grass exists to build a fairer internet where you can directly benefit from large corporations using your unused internet bandwidth.
Decentralized Earn Rewards
How does Grass actually work?
Training an AI model takes a huge amount of web data, which can be obtained from public websites. When you install a Grass node, the network will occasionally route web requests using your internet connection — think of it as your computer checking a random website every once in a while.

Grass nodes view these public websites and compile structured datasets from their contents, which are then sold to AI labs for use in the training process. None of this will be visible to you and it will not affect your internet experience at all.
Who builds Grass?
Wynd Labs is a core contributor to Grass. The Grass Foundation oversees the long-term direction of the network, token distribution, and governance, while Wynd Labs handles core protocol development and infrastructure.
Who uses the bandwidth I share?
Only verified institutions use your unused internet bandwidth. Your bandwidth may be used for tasks like checking local prices, viewing regional advertisements, or conducting academic research — all entirely safe, public-web activities.
Is Grass legal?
Yes, it is entirely legal and entirely safe. The network is used for scraping AI training data from publicly accessible websites only. Nothing remotely unsavory occurs when it does this. Grass is certified by industry-leading cybersecurity organizations like AppEsteem, ensuring your data and privacy are protected through world-class security standards.
Will Grass slow down my internet?
No. Grass only uses unused bandwidth — the portion of your internet connection that would otherwise sit idle. It automatically dials things back if your network is busy, so your browsing, streaming, and gaming will never be affected.
How do I sign up?
Simply create a username, enter your email, and set a password to sign up. Once you download the app, just log in and your Grass account is up and running. Download Grass on the Chrome Web Store or the Google Play store. Yes, it's that easy — with just three clicks you can start earning.
Earning & Points
6 questions
What are Grass Points?
Grass Points track your contributions to the Grass Network. They come in two forms:

Uptime Points — earned for staying connected and bringing others into the network through referrals.

Network Points — earned when your internet connection is actively used to handle traffic. Each day, a fixed pool of 1,000,000 Network Points is distributed across users according to the share of bandwidth they contribute.

Together, Grass Points track your participation in and contributions to the Grass Network and are one of the factors used to calculate the Grass Tokens you may receive in future distributions.
Uptime Points Network Points
How are Network Points calculated?
Each day, 1,000,000 Network Points are distributed proportionally based on bandwidth contributed. For example: if the Grass Network routes 100,000 GB of web traffic in a single day across 500,000 active users, and your device contributed 10 GB, you provided 0.01% of the day's bandwidth — earning approximately 100 Network Points.

Factors that influence how often your bandwidth is used include your geographic location, connection stability, and whether your connection has been recently used in prior tasks.
How does the referral program work?
When you join Grass, you'll get a unique referral link. Share it with others and earn bonus Uptime Points based on a tiered structure:

Primary Referrals: Your Uptime Points increase by 20% of your referred user's Uptime Points indefinitely.

Secondary Referrals: When someone you invited shares their link, you earn an additional 10% of that person's Uptime Points.

Tertiary Referrals: The third level earns you 5% of those users' Uptime Points.

You also receive 2,500 bonus points when your referral hits 100 hours of uptime, and new users who join via an invite link receive 5,000 bonus points.
Can I use Grass on multiple devices?
Yes, you can download Grass on multiple devices. However, if they're all connected to the same Wi-Fi network, the 24 hours of daily uptime will be split between all accounts on that network rather than each device receiving full uptime credit.
What factors affect my earnings?
Several factors influence how much bandwidth traffic is routed through your connection:

• Geographic location — certain regions handle more traffic depending on where web data requests originate.
• Connection stability — the strength and consistency of your connection affects whether it gets selected.
• Prior usage — if your connection was recently used, the network may prioritize other users' connections to maintain operational reliability.

You don't need to change anything to start earning — simply keeping your device connected and online maximizes your share.
Are there any restrictions on using Grass?
A few simple rules apply:

• You must be 18 or older to use Grass.
• Each user is permitted only one account. You can add multiple devices under your account, but attempting to game the referral program via multiple accounts risks a ban.
• Grass cannot be used with a VPN enabled.
• If two or more accounts share the same Wi-Fi network, daily uptime earnings are split between them.

That's it — if you're on a residential network, you're good to go!
Privacy & Safety
3 questions
Does Grass access my personal data?
No. Grass does not access or utilize your personal data in any way. The network is used to access public websites only — it has nothing to do with the data on your computer. We never access or use your browsing history, and we do not access or sell your personal information.

Your personal information is never accessible through the Grass network.
How does Grass protect my security?
Grass is certified by industry-leading cybersecurity organizations like AppEsteem, ensuring your data and privacy are protected through world-class security standards. All traffic routed through network relays is encrypted and metered for security and performance.

Grass only allows verified institutions to use your unused internet bandwidth for safe activities such as price comparison and academic research.
Encrypted Traffic AppEsteem Certified
Is my home network isolated from Grass traffic?
Yes. Grass only handles encrypted Grass traffic and is physically isolated from your personal devices, files, and browsing. No data from your home network is ever accessed or stored. The Grass node only routes public web traffic and cannot see or interact with anything on your local network.
Account & Setup
3 questions
How do I get started with Grass?
Getting started takes just three clicks:

1. Download the Grass app from the Chrome Web Store or Google Play store.
2. Sign up — create a username, enter your email, and set a password.
3. Log in and your Grass account is live and earning.

Grass handles everything automatically after that. You can pause sharing at any time.
Can I have multiple accounts?
No. Each user is permitted only one Grass account. You are able to add more than one device under your single account — for example, a computer and a mobile device. However, attempting to game the referral program by referring yourself under multiple accounts risks a permanent ban.
Can I use Grass while using a VPN?
No. Grass cannot be used with a VPN enabled. The network relies on residential IP addresses to route authentic geographic web requests for AI data collection. VPN addresses are datacenter IPs which are not eligible for the Grass network.
Tokens & Airdrops
4 questions
What are Grass Tokens?
Grass Tokens are the rewards you earn for sharing your unused internet bandwidth with the network. GRASS is the native token of the Grass Network.

They reflect your participation and contribution to building the network, giving you a way to own a part of the infrastructure you help power. GRASS is also staked to routers to ensure they remain in compliance with network protocols.
Native Token Governance
When are Grass Tokens distributed?
Grass Tokens are rewarded via airdrop at intervals determined by the Grass Foundation. The first airdrop occurred on October 28, 2024, during which 100,000,000 tokens were airdropped to more than 2,000,000 Grass users.

Users who were eligible for the initial airdrop but did not submit their public wallet address before the deadline will be given the opportunity to participate in future airdrops.
What's the relationship between Grass Points and Grass Tokens?
Grass Points track your contributions to the network over time — both your uptime availability and actual bandwidth usage. They are one of the factors used to calculate the Grass Tokens you may receive in future distributions.

Think of Points as your contribution ledger, and Tokens as the reward that flows from that ledger at airdrop intervals. Maximizing your Points by staying connected and referring others directly improves your Token eligibility.
I missed the first airdrop deadline — what now?
Users who were eligible to receive Grass Tokens in the initial airdrop but did not participate — either because they didn't submit their public wallet address before the deadline — will be given the opportunity to participate in future airdrops. Keep your node running and your account active to remain eligible for upcoming distributions.
